Almost 4 Years With You

Let me take you back...

1

Year 1 — The Beginning

It actually started on Racheal’s birthday. If you had told me then that you would become my best friend, I honestly wouldn’t have believed you. I didn’t even think you would invite me in the first place, I figured it was probably just because of Racheal. But even back then, I always thought you were cool. My mum even got to know you too. Prime Stober class days. Funny thing is, we didn’t even talk that much at the time. But somehow you were always there, someone I could come to and rant to about whatever was going on in my life. And then later, when you and Maishai started talking too, things somehow just kept unfolding from there.

2

Year 2 — The Growth

Ms Tosinnn. That woman was something. This was really the year our friendship took off. Things between us just became different. I was honestly surprised you stayed, even after you found out I cheated on Racheal. If you had decided to completely cut me off then, I honestly wouldn’t have blamed you. But you didn’t. And looking back, that year was… something else. Peak of my hormones 😭 But somehow, through all of that chaos, you still stayed. This was also when our prefectship started, always leaving class early like we had important government meetings. Then came the Joys era. Then the Maishai era. The Maishai era ended. The Joys era… ended a bit more dramatically. But through all of it, one thing stayed constant, we talked a lot. Like seriously, we started texting like crazy that year. And somewhere in the middle of all that, you even became my school mum. Omo… what a time that was

3

Year 3 — The Strength

This was the year our friendship really became solid. The year I could confidently beat my chest anywhere and say, “Yes, Michelle is my best friend.” Our back seat conversations alone could fill a whole book. I’ve honestly never felt as close to, or as genuinely at peace around anyone else the way I felt around you. I still remember one day when you told me, “I was about to place my head on your shoulder but then I realized you’re actually a guy.” That moment alone showed just how comfortable and natural our friendship had become. Then our prefect era was in full force. Leaving class early like VIPs and doing road safety work. And then there was that time I organized football and somehow got accused of being a pimp and organizing fornication. cough cough… Maishai. Guy, the way I would run away from Tofunmi just to come and stay with you 😭. And the funniest part was you helping me create excuses for leaving her and coming to meet you. Even when you and your girls were having your conversations, I was still there, just floating inside the gist like one honorary member. Then came the JAMB, WAEC, and NECO era. Thinking about it now, it’s crazy that we survived that period. There were moments we both felt like giving up, but somehow we always ended up motivating each other. You especially always found ways to prove yourself wrong and outperform your own expectations. It showed me something about you, whenever you truly put your mind to something, you always pull through. We even studied together sometimes. What an era. Then prom season came… and my hormones almost wanted to misbehave again. Thank God that phase has passed now 😭. Then there was Diane’s whole promposal, which you literally helped me prepare. In fact, you were basically the queen of promposal preparations. Then Zimuzo entered the picture too. Guy… what a time 🤣 Before we knew it, Prom came. Graduation came. One of the main reasons I was actually emotional on graduation day… was because of you. That was when we even came up with the best friend contract. All because deep down, we were both scared of losing this friendship.

4

Year 4 — Growing, But Still Us

Then university came along, and life started to become a bit more serious. Your preparations for school started too, learning German, attending classes, planning your future. We were both genuinely busy, and for a while we slowly drifted a little. But even during that time, I was always so proud of whatever you were doing. I remember how happy I was when you travelled. Seeing you experience new things and step into new opportunities genuinely made me proud. Then you came back and came to see us in Nile. I can’t even lie, I was genuinely joyful that day. Just seeing you doing your thing, growing as a person, actually finding a direction in life… it always made me happy. I’ve always been one of your biggest supporters, for real. And the funny thing is, even when we go a while without talking, once we start again it’s like nothing ever changed. We just click immediately. And honestly, I’m really glad we never grew apart. Because the truth is, I don’t think I can ever build a friendship like this with someone else again. The level of comfort, honesty, and understanding we have… it’s rare. For a long time after secondary school, I was scared that we might slowly drift away like most friendships do. But now? Seeing that we’ve actually survived this whole post–secondary school phase… that fear has finally calmed down. Because now I know something for sure: No matter where life takes us, we’ll always be there for each other.
